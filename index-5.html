Mission-driven product leader and Doctor of Engineering, advancing secure, accountable AI at the intersection of innovation, policy, and public trust.

My work accelerates adoption across regulated sectors, enabling government and healthcare organizations to innovate with confidence, compliance, and speed.

This chapter brought policy and practice together in real time. Insights from Harvard and MIT directly informed the development of Microsoft Security Copilot’s privacy-built-in, secure-by-design, and compliant-by-default capabilities—including the first previewed Security Agents—during a defining moment in the evolution of agentic AI.

Rooted in U.S. Navy service and shaped by advanced degrees from Harvard Kennedy School and The George Washington University, I bring engineering depth and policy fluency to the design of resilient, privacy-aware AI systems aligned with frameworks like FedRAMP, CMMC, EU AI Act, HIPAA, and ISO 42001.

As an educator and mentor, I translate complex cybersecurity and AI risks into actionable insights—promoting ethical innovation and fostering a culture of accountability across emerging technologies.
